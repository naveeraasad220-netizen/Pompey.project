# What is Pompey?
A handbuilt underwater marine camera device — my first ever hardware project.
Pompey is a underwater camera device shaped like a semi-circular body with a triangular shape attached to the end of it in. It is designed to cut through water easily and sharply as it decends. You can lower it to a max of 30 feet into the sea with the help of a braided string to photograph marin animals, only clicking when something moves with the help of red LEDs all without causing disturbance to underwater creatures.
# How do you use Pompey?
You simply lower the device to your desired length underwater holding it there for sometime, while the device itselves clicks pictures with it red LED lights, and automatically stops when it senses itself getting pulled up!
# Why am I making it?
Ever since I was a child, I have always been drawn to the ocean, what goes on in it, what lives in it, and how their lives look like. That curiousity has always stuck with me. So, when I decided to shoot my shot at hack club's fallout I instantly knew what I needed to make. I knew that only my passion and my love for something would push me to extend levels, and keep me going!

My love didn't end at the ocean, I also admire its animals. I wanted to build something that could observe the underwater world without disturbing it's residents. No flashing white lights, no loud motors, no large foreign object crashing into the water. Just a quiet device that descends slowly, waits, and captures whatever decides to swim by.

This is my first hardware project — so I want to keep the design simple, not complicated and contain nothing unnecessary.
